Sol’s system and its neighbors v2.3.0

 

First why “Sol” instead of “sun” ? "Sol is the name of our sun”. If you ask a room full of aliens what they think of the sun, you will get a different answer from each one, depending on the star that their home planet orbits.If you ask them about Sol, you are asking specifically about the star that the Earth orbits, which you can also refer to as Sol III to get inline with most designations they give to planets that are encountered..". “Sol” is used in many SF books and television series.

Tap on one of the planetary bodies and you will get information about it : Aphelion,Perihelion,Semi-major axis,Orbital eccentricity, Orbital period,Average orbital speed,Inclination to ecliptic, Number of moons (with their names), Mean radius,Mass, Type of planet, Ring system, Mean density, Surface gravity,Escape velocity, Sideral rotation period, Axial tilt, Discovery, Surface pressure,Temperature, Atmospheric composition, Apparent magnitude, Albedo (geometric), Global magnetic field, Communication delay to Earth (min and max)

Tap on an information and you will get its definition

This database containing all the stars from the Hipparcos, Yale Bright Star, and Gliese catalogs (119,613 stars)

All stars have both an epoch and equinox of 2000.0


  1. - The Magnitude is the star's apparent visual magnitude.


  1. -Distance : Tap on Distance and you’ll get the stars sorted by distance. Tapping again will alternate between parsec and light year. A value of 10000000 indicates missing or dubious value


- RA / Dec : Right Ascension & Declination - Tap on RA / Dec and you’ll get the stars sorted by Right Ascension & Declination. Tapping again and you will get a RA picker


  1. -Magnitude : Tap on Magnitude and you’ll get the stars sorted by magnitude (apparent).


  1. -Name : Tap on “Name proper” and you’ll get the stars sorted by name. Tapping again will give you the Bayer/Flamssteed designation, the star ID from the Gliese, the Hipparcos Catalog of Nearby Stars and again the proper name.


You can restrict the number of stars by magnitude. Tap on <  and  >
