Hydrodynamics 101

 

Particles (molecules of air) just collide with the car.

Emitter(s) and the car are 3D figures.

Particles collide with the car and with the other particles. Increasing the number of the particles would give a flow closer to reality (back). But my poor 2Core Duo is not up to the task. 

(15 hours for this render) - The red emitter is smaller than the width of the car so you don’t see them much on the side.

Less particles (2,000 vs 12,000) & the result is better ! Why?

I added a TP Tube , a TP Planar Wind and a TP Nozzle.

The Tube to constrain the particles along the x-axis, and the TP Nozzle to push them back together at the back of the car. Another TP Tube at the end to cancel the effect of the Nozzle (remove the y-axis movement). You can get a similar result without the particles colliding with each other just by tweaking the TP effects. Or you can cheat even more just by having the particles follow a spline for their course without even colliding with the car.

(30 mins for this render)

Cinema 4D R11.5 - TP Standard Emitter, TP Particle Constraint, TP Planar Wind, TP Nozzle, TP Tube . So lot of XPresso.
